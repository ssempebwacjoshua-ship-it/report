# School Connect Smart Pages School Onboarding Guide

## What the School Must Provide Before Setup

- School name and official contacts.
- One main school representative for approvals.
- Sample documents for testing.
- Any preferred branding text or logo.
- Any preferred colors, header text, or footer text.
- A clear decision on who will review documents before release.

## Example Documents for the First Test

Use a small set of real school documents:

- School notice
- Circular
- Timetable
- Programme
- Meeting minutes
- Exam schedule

## Document Quality Requirements

- Clear scan or photo.
- Complete page visible.
- Text must be readable.
- Page must be the correct way up.
- Avoid blurry, cropped, shadowed, or tilted images.

Good source quality makes extraction and template output much better.

## Branding Requirements

If the school wants branded output, provide:

- Logo
- School name spelling
- Color preference
- Header text
- Footer text
- Any standard wording for notices or circulars

## User Training Plan

1. Train staff on where Smart Pages lives in the app.
1. Show how to upload a document.
1. Show how to review extracted text.
1. Show how to correct errors before generating output.
1. Show how to print, download, and publish.
1. Explain the difference between Smart Pages and Report Lab.

## First Week Rollout Checklist

1. Confirm the school account can log in.
1. Confirm Smart Pages opens correctly.
1. Upload one notice.
1. Upload one circular.
1. Upload one timetable or programme.
1. Review the extracted content with the school representative.
1. Generate and print one finished document.
1. Test the publish link if the school wants public sharing.

## After Rollout

Once the school is live:

- Keep a human review step before publishing.
- Use collections for repeating record sets.
- Use bulk generation only after the school has agreed on the document format.
- Escalate unclear documents to the support team instead of guessing.
